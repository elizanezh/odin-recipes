Sesame-Seared Tuna

This sesame-seared tuna is an easy, great-tasting dish. Fresh tuna steaks are coated with sesame seeds, then quickly seared and served rare, so be sure to use good quality fresh tuna.

Prep Time:

10 mins

Cooking Time:

1 mins

Total Time:

11 mins

Servings:

4

Yield:

4 steaks

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine vinegar
  • 1/2 cup sesame seeds
  • 4 (6 ounce) tuna steaks
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • wasabi pastewasabi paste

Directions

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Stir together soy sauce, sesame oil, mirin, and honey in a small bowl
  3. Pour half into a separate small bowl, stir in rice wine vinegar, and set it aside as a dipping sauce.
  4. Spread sesame seeds out on a plate. Coat tuna steaks with remaining soy sauce mixture, then press into sesame seeds to coat.
  5. Heat olive oil in a cast iron skillet over high heat until very hot. Place steaks in the pan; sear for about 30 seconds on each side.
  6. Serve with dipping sauce and wasabi paste.
